Cybersecurity Risk Assessment & Threat Prioritization

SOC-focused risk evaluation, prioritization, and mitigation planning

Overview

In this project, I conducted a cybersecurity risk assessment in a simulated organizational environment to identify potential threats, evaluate their likelihood and impact, and prioritize them based on risk level.

Key Takeaway: Risk assessment helps security teams focus on the threats most likely to cause real damage, improving prioritization and response.

Objective

  • Identify common cybersecurity threats
  • Evaluate likelihood and potential impact
  • Assign risk levels
  • Recommend mitigation strategies
  • Understand how risk influences SOC priorities

Key Findings

  • Phishing remains one of the highest-risk threats because it combines high likelihood with high impact.
  • Brute force attacks require strong authentication controls and monitoring.
  • Malware and insider threats require layered controls and visibility.
  • AI-related misuse is an emerging high-risk area when sensitive data or unreliable outputs are involved.

Mitigation Strategies

  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
  • Enforce strong password policies
  • Deploy phishing detection and email filtering
  • Use logging and continuous monitoring
  • Apply least privilege access controls
  • Establish policies for responsible AI usage

SOC Analyst Relevance

Risk assessment supports SOC workflows by helping analysts prioritize alerts, focus on high-impact threats, and reduce alert fatigue through better triage decisions.

What I Learned

This project reinforced that not all threats carry equal risk. Effective security depends on prioritizing high-impact threats and applying layered controls to reduce overall risk before incidents escalate.
