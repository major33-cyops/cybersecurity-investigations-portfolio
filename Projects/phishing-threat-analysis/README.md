# Phishing Threat Analysis

## Overview
In this project, I analyzed phishing threats and identified the warning signs that help users and analysts recognize suspicious emails, fake websites, and social engineering attempts. The goal was to understand how phishing works, how to detect it, and what actions should be taken to reduce risk.

**Note:** Developed as part of a cybersecurity internship using a simulated environment.

## Key Threat Indicators Evaluated

### Email Red Flags
- Spoofed or mismatched sender addresses
- Misspelled or lookalike domains
- Urgent or threatening language
- Unexpected attachments or links
- Requests for passwords or sensitive data

### Suspicious Website Red Flags
- Misspelled URLs
- No HTTPS
- Fake login pages
- Poor formatting or broken links
- Requests for excessive personal information

---

## Phishing Types Identified
- Email phishing
- Spear phishing
- Whaling
- Smishing
- Vishing
- QR code phishing
- Business Email Compromise (BEC)

---

## Key Findings
- Phishing often relies on urgency, trust, and confusion rather than technical sophistication
- Email and website indicators can often reveal a phishing attempt before harm occurs
- Users are a major part of security defense, so awareness directly reduces risk
- Fast reporting helps reduce the impact of suspicious emails or links

---

## Response Guidance
If a phishing attempt is suspected:
1. Do not click links or open attachments
2. Preserve evidence with screenshots or saved messages
3. Report the message to the proper contact
4. If a link was clicked, notify support immediately
5. If credentials were entered, change passwords right away
6. Remove the message after documentation and reporting

---

## SOC Analyst Relevance
Phishing is one of the most common security issues handled by SOC teams.

SOC analysts must:
- Investigate suspicious emails
- Identify indicators of phishing
- Escalate confirmed threats
- Support containment after user interaction
- Help reduce repeat incidents through awareness and reporting

### Example SOC Impact
- Suspicious sender and link mismatch → possible phishing alert
- Credential submission after fake login page → possible account compromise
- Business email compromise attempt → fraud and escalation risk

Understanding phishing indicators helps analysts triage incidents faster and reduce user-based risk.
